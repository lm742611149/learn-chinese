面试技巧

Miànshì jìqiǎo
Interview Tips · 139 words · ~2 min
找zhǎo工作gōngzuò的de时候shíhou，面试miànshì常常chángcháng比bǐ简历jiǎnlì更gèng重要zhòngyào。
When looking for a job, the interview is often more important than the resume.
很多hěn duō人rén能力nénglì不错búcuò，却què因为yīnwèi太tài紧张jǐnzhāng，面试miànshì的de时候shíhou一yí句jù话huà也yě说shuō不好bù hǎo。
Many people are quite capable, yet because they get too nervous, they can't get a single sentence out well in the interview.
其实qíshí，面试miànshì是shì有yǒu技巧jìqiǎo的de。
Actually, there are techniques to interviewing.
首先shǒuxiān，去qù之前zhīqián要yào了解liǎojiě这zhè家jiā公司gōngsī，知道zhīdào他们tāmen需要xūyào什么样shénmeyàng的de人rén。
First, before you go, learn about the company and find out what kind of person they need.
其次qícì，回答huídá问题wèntí的de时候shíhou，不要búyào只zhǐ说shuō“我wǒ很hěn努力nǔlì”，最好zuìhǎo举jǔ一yí个ge真实zhēnshí的de例子lìzi。
Second, when answering questions, don't just say "I work very hard" — it's best to give a real example.
另外lìngwài，穿chuān得de干净gānjìng一点儿yìdiǎnr，早zǎo到dào十shí分钟fēnzhōng，这些zhèxiē小事xiǎoshì都dōu会huì给gěi人rén好hǎo印象yìnxiàng。
In addition, dress neatly and arrive ten minutes early — these small things all leave a good impression.
最后zuìhòu别bié忘wàng了le：面试miànshì不仅bùjǐn是shì公司gōngsī在zài选xuǎn你nǐ，而且érqiě是shì你nǐ在zài选xuǎn公司gōngsī。
Finally, don't forget: an interview is not only the company choosing you, but also you choosing the company.
放松fàngsōng一点儿yìdiǎnr，把bǎ面试miànshì当成dàngchéng一yí次cì聊天liáotiān，你nǐ会huì做zuò得de更gèng好hǎo。
Relax a little, treat the interview as a chat, and you will do even better.

Grammar note 语法点

首先……其次……另外……最后……

A set of connectors for listing points in order: 首先 (first), 其次 (second), 另外 (in addition), 最后 (finally). The text uses them to organize the interview tips.

首先要多听，其次要多说，另外还要多写。— First listen more, second speak more, and in addition write more.
不仅……而且……

"Not only A, but also B" joins two related points, the second often more important: 面试不仅是公司在选你，而且是你在选公司。

运动不仅对身体好，而且让人心情好。— Exercise is not only good for the body, but also lifts your mood.

Check yourself 小测验

1. According to the text, what is often more important than the resume?
2. When answering questions, what should you do instead of just saying "I work very hard"?
3. What does the author say an interview really is?
