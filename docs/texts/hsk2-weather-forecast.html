天气预报

Tiānqì yùbào
The Weather Forecast · 98 words · ~2 min
每天měitiān晚上wǎnshang，我wǒ都dōu看kàn天气tiānqì预报yùbào。
Every evening, I watch the weather forecast.
昨天zuótiān电视diànshì里lǐ说shuō：“明天míngtiān有yǒu雨yǔ，会huì很hěn冷lěng。”
Yesterday the TV said, "Tomorrow there will be rain, and it will be very cold."
今天jīntiān早上zǎoshang出门chūmén的de时候shíhou，天tiān是shì阴yīn的de，我wǒ穿chuān了le很hěn多duō衣服yīfu。
This morning when I left home, the sky was overcast, so I put on a lot of clothes.
但是dànshì中午zhōngwǔ没有méiyǒu下雨xiàyǔ，天tiān晴qíng了le，还hái很hěn热rè！
But at noon it didn't rain — the sky turned sunny, and it was even hot!
我wǒ穿chuān得de太tài多duō，走zǒu一点儿yìdiǎnr路lù就jiù热rè得de不bù想xiǎng说话shuōhuà。
I was wearing too much; after walking just a little I was so hot I didn't even want to talk.
我wǒ笑xiào着zhe想xiǎng：天气tiānqì预报yùbào有时候yǒushíhou也yě会huì错cuò。
I smiled and thought: sometimes the weather forecast can be wrong too.
明天míngtiān我wǒ还hái会huì看kàn天气tiānqì预报yùbào，但是dànshì我wǒ也yě要yào看看kànkan天tiān！
Tomorrow I will still watch the weather forecast, but I will also take a look at the sky myself!

Grammar note 语法点

会 (prediction)

会 before an adjective or verb can mean "will (probably)": 明天会很冷。

明天会很冷。 — Tomorrow it will be very cold.
Adjective + 得 + result

得 after an adjective shows how far it goes: 热得不想说话 (so hot that ...).

我热得不想说话。 — I was so hot that I didn't want to talk.

Check yourself 小测验

1. What did the weather forecast say about today?
2. What was the weather actually like at noon?
3. What does the narrator decide to do from now on?
